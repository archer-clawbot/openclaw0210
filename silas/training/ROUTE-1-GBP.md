## SPEC-001: GBP SERVICES

### Core Concept
Every custom service entry is a micro-landing page, not a keyword stub. Detailed service entries match high-intent long-tail queries and trigger "justification snippets" in the Local Pack.

### Procedure
1. Research client's full service catalog + competitor service entries
2. For each service, write a detailed description (300-1,000 characters) that:
   - Names the specific service in the first sentence
   - Describes what the service includes
   - Mentions the outcome/benefit to the customer
   - Includes geographic modifiers naturally
   - Reads like a mini sales page, not a keyword list
3. Group services into logical categories (max 10 categories recommended)
4. Add pricing where appropriate (price transparency = higher CTR)

### Template
```
Service Name: [Specific Long-Tail Service Name]
Category: [Service Category]
Description: [Business Name] provides [specific service] for [target customer] 
in [city/area]. Our [service] includes [specific details of what's included]. 
[Outcome statement]. [Credential or trust signal]. Serving [neighborhoods/areas].
Price: [Range or "Contact for quote"]
```

### Scoring (0-10)
- 0: No custom services
- 3: Generic keyword-only services
- 5: Some detailed, some generic
- 7: All services detailed with descriptions
- 10: Every service is a micro-landing page targeting high-intent long-tails

## SPEC-002: GBP DESCRIPTION ENGINEERING

### Core Concept ‚Äî The "Local Hub Gambit"
The 750-character GBP description uses entity co-citations to signal that the business is a connected local hub, not an isolated listing. Mentioning verifiable local entities (suppliers, associations, landmarks, builders) creates semantic relationships that Google validates.

### Structure (750 characters max)
```
[Opening identity statement with primary keyword + city]
[Entity co-citation #1 ‚Äî local supplier, partner, or association]
[Entity co-citation #2 ‚Äî landmark, neighborhood, or institution]
[Core services with specific details]
[Credential/trust signals ‚Äî years, certifications, awards]
[Service area with specific neighborhoods]
[Closing CTA]
```

### Entity Co-Citation Categories
- **Suppliers/Partners:** Local suppliers the business actually uses (verifiable)
- **Professional Associations:** Chamber of Commerce, industry groups, BBB
- **Geographic Anchors:** Landmarks, neighborhoods, shopping centers, highways
- **Complementary Businesses:** Other local businesses that serve same customers

### Rules
- Every entity mentioned MUST be real and verifiable by Google
- Never fabricate relationships ‚Äî Google cross-checks
- Front-load the primary keyword + city in the first sentence
- No phone numbers, URLs, or promotional language in description
- Write in third person ("Business Name provides..." not "We provide...")

### Scoring (0-10)
- 0: Empty or auto-generated
- 3: Basic description, no entity co-citations
- 5: Keyword-optimized but missing authority signals
- 7: Entity co-citations + credentials + geo-modifiers
- 10: Full Local Hub Gambit with 3+ verifiable entity co-citations

## SPEC-003: GBP Q&A PRE-EMPTION

### Core Concept
Seed 15-30 Q&A entries before customers ask random questions. Each answer is a keyword-dense content block that Google indexes and uses for relevance signals.

### Procedure
1. Generate questions from keyword research (what people actually search)
2. Write comprehensive answers (150-500 characters each)
3. Post questions from a Google account, then answer as the business owner
4. Include natural keyword variations in answers
5. For complex topics, link to a Google Doc "answer funnel" (public doc with detailed guide)

### Question Categories
- **Service-specific:** "Do you offer [specific service] in [city]?"
- **Pricing:** "How much does [service] cost?"
- **Process:** "What's the process for [service]?"
- **Credentials:** "Are you licensed/insured/certified for [service]?"
- **Area:** "Do you serve [neighborhood/city]?"
- **Comparison:** "What's the difference between [option A] and [option B]?"

### Answer Template
```
Q: [Question matching a real search query]
A: Yes, [Business Name] offers [service] in [city/area]. [2-3 sentences with 
specific details ‚Äî pricing, process, timeline, or qualifications]. 
[For more details: link to Google Doc guide if applicable]. 
Contact us at [phone] to [CTA].
```

### Google Docs Answer Funnel
For complex questions that exceed Q&A character limits:
1. Create a comprehensive Google Doc (2,000+ words, publicly viewable)
2. Link from Q&A answer: "We created a free guide: [Doc link]"
3. This creates a Google-to-Google trust signal (GBP ‚Üí Google Docs)

### Scoring (0-10)
- 0: No Q&A entries
- 3: Few generic Q&A, thin answers
- 5: 10-15 entries, moderate keyword density
- 7: 20+ entries, comprehensive answers, all service areas covered
- 10: 30+ entries with answer funnels, covering every service + area + FAQ

## SPEC-004: GBP PRODUCTS TAB

### Core Concept
Products tab is a visual conversion tool. Each product entry has an image, description, price, and CTA button ‚Äî functioning as a visual menu of offerings.

### Procedure
1. Create product collections (categories) matching service categories
2. For each product/service:
   - Professional image (real photos preferred over stock)
   - Descriptive title (service name, not generic)
   - Detailed description (features, benefits, what's included)
   - Price or price range
   - CTA button (Call, Learn More, Book)
3. Prioritize highest-margin or most-searched services first

### Template
```
Collection: [Service Category]
Product Name: [Specific Service Name]
Description: [What it includes, who it's for, key benefit, timeframe]
Price: $X,XXX or $X,XXX - $X,XXX or "Request Quote"
CTA: [Call / Learn More / Book Online]
Image: [Professional photo of this service in action]
```

### Scoring (0-10)
- 0: Products tab empty
- 3: Few products, no images or descriptions
- 5: Products listed but generic descriptions
- 7: All services as products with images and descriptions
- 10: Complete product catalog with professional images, pricing, compelling descriptions

## SPEC-005: GBP POSTING STRATEGY

### Core Concept ‚Äî Pre-Emptive Framing
Posts seed content into Google's index and AI systems. The strategy isn't promotional ‚Äî it's about framing the business as the authority in its market by defining what quality means, then demonstrating the business meets those criteria.

### Cadence
3 posts per week minimum. Schedule:
- **Monday:** Service/expertise highlight
- **Wednesday:** Community/local engagement
- **Friday:** Offer, event, or seasonal content

### Rules
- Every post must include a CTA (call, visit, book)
- Include at least one geo-modifier per post
- Vary keywords across posts (don't repeat the same keyword every post)
- Include a photo with every post (real > stock)
- Never use GBP posts for link building or thin content

- 5: 1-2 posts/week, some keyword optimization
