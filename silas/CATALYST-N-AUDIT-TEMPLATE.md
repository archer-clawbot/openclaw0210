## TEMPLATE USAGE RULES FOR SILAS

1. **Never skip sections.** If you can't get data, say so. Don't omit.
2. **Always use web_search** for SERP data, backlink estimates, competitor analysis, and content gap identification. Never estimate what you can verify.
3. **Scores must have justifications.** A naked number is meaningless to a client.
4. **The Priority Matrix must reference NSPECs.** Every action ties back to an NSPEC-XXX.
5. **The Dispatch Queue is for Archer.** It tells him exactly what to route where after the audit is approved.
6. **Scribe briefs must be detailed.** National content requires more specificity than local. Every brief needs target keyword, intent, word count, SERP competitors to beat, and subtopics. Vague briefs waste revision cycles.
7. **Client-facing sections:** 1-3, 8, 12-14. Internal sections: 4-7, 9-11. When producing a "client-ready" report, include everything but note which sections are for internal strategy review.
8. **File naming:** `audits/[business-slug]-catalyst-n-audit-[YYYY-MM-DD].md`
9. **Overall score weighting:** Route 1 (25%) + Route 2 (25%) + Route 3 (20%) + Route 4 (20%) + Route 5 (5%) + AI/XP (5%).
10. **Do not use APEX local signals.** No GBP scoring, no citation audits, no geo-grid references, no map pack tracking, no location silos, no grounding boxes. These are APEX-only concepts. If you find yourself writing about GBP categories or NAP consistency in a CATALYST-N audit, you've loaded the wrong methodology.

## METHODOLOGY DIFFERENCES: CATALYST-N vs. APEX

| Dimension | APEX (Local) | CATALYST-N (National) |
|-----------|-------------|----------------------|
| Primary ranking lever | GBP signals + proximity | Topical authority + backlinks |
| Content strategy | City/location pages, service pages | Topic clusters, pillar content, comprehensive resources |
| Off-site focus | Citations + NAP consistency | Backlink quality + referring domain diversity |
