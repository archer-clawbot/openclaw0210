## RISK ANALYSIS

### Potential Objections to This Strategy:

#### "Our customers won't pay these prices"
**Response:** They will—if the value is there. Right now you're competing on price because you're not selling value. Four Seasons charges premium prices and has 180,000 customers. Price is only an issue in the absence of value.

#### "The 5-year guarantee is too risky"
**Response:** 
- Most electrical work doesn't fail if done correctly
- Your current warranty already covers workmanship
- Annual inspections catch issues before they become expensive
- Insurance can cover catastrophic claims
- Refund rate typically 2-5% in service industries
- Guarantee becomes a sales tool that pays for itself in higher closing rates

#### "We don't have time to create all this"
**Response:** 
- Start with one package (Upgrade tier)
- Test with 10 customers before full rollout
- Outsource website updates to Scribe
- Use templates for sales scripts
- Implementation can happen in phases

#### "What if we can't deliver on the guarantee?"
**Response:** Then you have a quality control problem that will sink you anyway. The guarantee forces operational excellence—which is good. It also identifies systemic issues early when they're cheaper to fix.
