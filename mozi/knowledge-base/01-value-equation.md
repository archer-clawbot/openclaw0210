# Value Equation

**Source:** $100M Offers, Chapter 6
**Category:** Pricing & Value
**Tags:** pricing, value, offer-design, perception

## Core Concept

The Value Equation is a formula that quantifies the perceived value of any offer. It determines what you can charge and whether prospects will say yes. Value is NOT about cost—it's about the gap between what something costs you and what you charge.

## Formula

```
                Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
VALUE = ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
              Perceived Time Delay × Perceived Effort & Sacrifice
```

## Variables

1. **Dream Outcome** (INCREASE) — The absolute best result your product/service delivers. Not the process, but the destination. "Sell the vacation, not the plane flight."
2. **Perceived Likelihood of Achievement** (INCREASE) — How likely the prospect believes THEY will actually get the result. Social proof, case studies, guarantees boost this.
3. **Perceived Time Delay** (DECREASE) — How long between purchase and result. Shorter = more valuable. Instant = infinitely valuable.
4. **Perceived Effort & Sacrifice** (DECREASE) — How much work/pain the prospect must endure. Less effort = more valuable. Zero effort = infinitely valuable.

## Key Insight: Get The Bottom To Zero

If you can make Time Delay and Effort & Sacrifice approach zero, value approaches infinity. This is why the best companies (Apple, Amazon, Netflix) focus on making things immediate, seamless, and effortless. Beginners focus on bigger promises (top of equation). Experts focus on reducing friction (bottom of equation).

## Key Insight: Perception Is Reality

It's not about actual improvement—it's about PERCEIVED improvement. The London tunnel system increased rider satisfaction more with a simple dot-map showing wait times (cost: millions) than by making trains faster (cost: billions).

## Decision Criteria

Use this framework when:
- Designing or evaluating any offer
- Setting prices (higher value = higher justified price)
- Diagnosing why an offer isn't converting
- Comparing your offer against competitors

## Application Template

For any offer, score each variable 1-10:
1. Dream Outcome: How desirable is the end result? ___/10
2. Likelihood of Achievement: How confident is the prospect? ___/10
3. Time Delay: How fast do they get results? (lower = better) ___/10
4. Effort & Sacrifice: How much work required? (lower = better) ___/10

Then identify: Which variable is weakest? That's your biggest lever for improvement.

## Pro Tips

- "Any fool can sell a product by offering it for a discount. It takes great marketing to sell the same product for a premium."
- Logical solutions have usually been tried. Look for PSYCHOLOGICAL solutions to increase perceived value.
- A 5x price increase with better value perception can 38x profit.
