# Grand Slam Offer

**Source:** $100M Offers, Chapters 2, 9-10
**Category:** Offer Design
**Tags:** offer-creation, packaging, differentiation, value-stacking

## Core Concept

A Grand Slam Offer is a combination of products, services, delivery vehicles, pricing, payment terms, and guarantees that makes your offering so compelling it becomes incomparable to anything else in the marketplace. Like a grand slam in baseball scores the maximum runs, a Grand Slam Offer scores maximum revenue with no additional effort beyond a normal offer.

## The 5-Step Creation Process

### Step 1: Identify Dream Outcome
Define the ultimate result your prospect wants. Not your product—their destination.
- Not "gym membership" → "Lose 20lbs in 6 weeks"
- Not "SEO services" → "Double your inbound leads in 90 days"
- Not "restaurant consulting" → "Fill every seat Friday-Sunday within 60 days"

### Step 2: List ALL Problems
Write down every obstacle, fear, and friction point the prospect faces. Think about what happens immediately BEFORE and AFTER they use your product. Use the Value Equation drivers as prompts:

For each step in the customer journey, ask:
1. **Dream Outcome concerns:** "This won't be financially worth it"
2. **Likelihood concerns:** "It won't work for me specifically / I can't stick with it / External factors will interfere"
3. **Effort concerns:** "This will be too hard / confusing / I'll hate it / I'll suck at it"
4. **Time concerns:** "This will take too long / I'm too busy / It won't be convenient"

Goal: Generate 30-60+ specific problems. More problems = more value you can create.

### Step 3: Turn Problems Into Solutions
Transform each problem into solution-oriented language. Formula: "How to [reverse the problem]"
- Problem: "Buying healthy food is expensive" → Solution: "How to buy healthy food for less than your current grocery bill"
- Problem: "I don't know what keywords to target" → Solution: "How to find the exact keywords your customers are searching for in under 10 minutes"

### Step 4: Determine Delivery Vehicles
For each solution, decide HOW to deliver it. Options include:
- 1-on-1 (highest value, highest cost)
- Small group
- 1-to-many (courses, templates, tools)
- DIY with support
- Done-for-you
- Done-with-you

Prioritize "one-to-many" solutions: high one-time creation cost, infinitely low additional cost. Software, templates, recorded training, tools, calculators.

### Step 5: Trim & Stack
- **Trim:** Remove high-cost/low-value items first, then low-cost/low-value items
- **Stack:** Bundle remaining high-value items into themed packages with sexy names
- **Price anchoring:** Assign individual values to each bundle, then offer the total at a fraction

Use the Value Equation to evaluate what remains:
1. Will they financially value this?
2. Will it make them believe they'll succeed?
3. Will it reduce their perceived effort?
4. Will it reduce their perceived time to result?

## The Sales-to-Fulfillment Continuum

There's always a tradeoff between ease of selling and ease of fulfilling. The goal is the sweet spot. Hormozi's mantra: "Create flow. Monetize flow. Then add friction."

Start by over-delivering (easy to sell, hard to fulfill) → Get cash flowing → Optimize delivery for efficiency.

## The Bundle Formula (Example Output)

```
Problem → Solution Wording → Sexy Bundle Name ... with benefit statement ($X value)
   - Delivery item 1
   - Delivery item 2
   - Delivery item 3
```

Total stacked value: $X,XXX → Your price: $XXX (fraction of total value)

## Key Results

- Makes price-based comparison impossible
- Shifts prospect from price-driven to value-driven decision
- Creates conviction in what you're selling (you believe it too)
- Solves ALL perceived problems, not just some

## Decision Criteria

Use when:
- Launching a new product/service
- Current offer feels commoditized or price-sensitive
- Competitors are undercutting on price
- Close rates are declining
- Need to justify premium pricing
