# Polypropylene Sandbags: Why They're the Industry Standard for Flood Protection

Walk into any hardware store before a hurricane or check the supply list from your local emergency management office, and you'll almost certainly find polypropylene sandbags — the woven, synthetic bags that have become the dominant flood protection tool for homeowners, municipalities, and emergency responders alike. Polypropylene sandbags have largely replaced burlap as the go-to option, and for good reason.

This guide explains what polypropylene sandbags are, why they've become the standard, and how to choose, use, and store them effectively.

---

## What Are Polypropylene Sandbags?

Polypropylene (often abbreviated as PP) is a thermoplastic polymer — a type of plastic. When woven into thin, flat strips and interlocked in a grid pattern, polypropylene creates a strong, flexible, and lightweight fabric. That fabric is then cut and sewn into bags sized for sand or soil fill.

The result is a sandbag that is:
- Significantly stronger than burlap by weight
- Resistant to UV radiation (especially UV-treated PP bags)
- Resistant to moisture, mold, and mildew
- Capable of lasting years in outdoor storage
- Available in a wide range of sizes and colors

Polypropylene sandbags are available in both woven and non-woven configurations, though woven polypropylene is by far the most common for flood protection. The woven structure provides the strength needed to hold fill material under pressure while maintaining enough flexibility to conform to stacking surfaces.

---

## Key Properties of Polypropylene Sandbags

### Superior Strength-to-Weight Ratio

Polypropylene woven fabric is pound-for-pound significantly stronger than burlap. This means PP bags can hold the same volume of fill with less risk of tearing, seam failure, or rupture during deployment or under water pressure. This is particularly important when bags are being dragged, dropped, or stacked quickly during a flood emergency.

### UV Resistance

Standard polypropylene degrades under UV radiation — but most sandbags sold for flood protection include UV stabilizers in the polymer blend. UV-treated polypropylene sandbags can withstand months to years of outdoor exposure before significant degradation occurs. This is a major advantage over burlap, which begins showing UV damage within 3–6 months.

Always verify whether bags are UV-treated (also labeled "UV stabilized" or "UV resistant") when purchasing for outdoor or extended-duration applications.

### Moisture and Mold Resistance

Unlike natural fibers, polypropylene does not absorb water. The bags themselves remain structurally intact even after extended exposure to water, and they resist mold, mildew, and rot. This makes polypropylene sandbags substantially better for storage in less-than-perfect conditions (sheds, garages, basements) where some moisture exposure is likely.

### Non-Biodegradable

Polypropylene is a plastic and does not biodegrade in any meaningful timeframe. This is a significant environmental consideration. PP sandbags require proper disposal after use — they cannot be composted or left in place to naturally break down. Disposal costs and environmental impact should factor into your decision if you're choosing between PP and biodegradable alternatives. ## Polypropylene Sandbag Sizes and Specifications

Polypropylene sandbags are produced in a range of sizes. The most common standard sizes for flood protection:

| Size (Empty) | Typical Fill Weight | Primary Use |
|---|---|---|
| 14" x 26" | 35–40 lbs | Residential flood barriers |
| 18" x 30" | 55–65 lbs | Levees, larger barriers |
| 12" x 24" | 25–30 lbs | Lighter applications, tight spaces |
| 24" x 36" | 75–90 lbs | Heavy-duty commercial use |

The 14"x26" size is the most widely used for residential applications. It's the size distributed by most municipalities during flood events and the size most commonly stocked at hardware stores. ## How Long Do Polypropylene Sandbags Last?

| Condition | Expected Lifespan |
|---|---|
| Stored dry, indoors | 10+ years |
| UV-treated, outdoor exposure | 1–3 years |
| Non-UV-treated, outdoor exposure | 3–6 months |
| Filled and deployed in flood conditions | Several months |

Proper storage dramatically extends the life of polypropylene sandbags. Keep empty bags out of direct sunlight, in a cool dry location (a storage bin, shed, or interior room) to maximize shelf life. See our guide on [how to store sandbags](/how-to-store-sandbags/) for detailed storage recommendations.

## Woven Polypropylene vs. Non-Woven Polypropylene

Not all polypropylene sandbags are woven. There are two main types:

**Woven polypropylene**: The standard for flood protection. Strong, holds shape under fill pressure, allows minimal water seepage through the weave. Used for virtually all residential and municipal sandbag applications.

**Non-woven polypropylene**: Made from bonded fibers rather than woven strips. More like a fabric than a net. Typically used for filtration, erosion control fabrics, and geotextile applications rather than flood sandbags. Less common in flood protection.

For flood protection, woven polypropylene is the correct choice in almost all cases. Read our full comparison of [woven vs. non-woven sandbags](/woven-vs-non-woven-sandbags/).

## Polypropylene vs. Burlap Sandbags: Full Comparison

| Feature | Polypropylene | Burlap |
|---|---|---|
| Material | Synthetic plastic | Natural jute fiber |
| UV resistance | Good (UV-treated) to Excellent | Poor |
| Biodegradable | No | Yes |
| Moisture resistance | Excellent | Low |
| Strength | Very high | Moderate |
| Surface grip | Good | Excellent |
| Stored lifespan | 10+ years | 3–7 years |
| Outdoor lifespan | 1–3 years (UV-treated) | 3–6 months |
| Environmental disposal | Requires proper disposal | Compostable (if uncontaminated) |
| Typical cost | Similar | Similar or slightly higher |

For most homeowners and emergency managers, polypropylene sandbags offer better overall performance across more use cases. The key trade-off is environmental: PP sandbags are plastic and won't biodegrade. For applications where long-term soil contact and eventual breakdown is desirable (erosion control, slope stabilization), burlap or [jute sandbags](/jute-sandbags/) may be preferable.

## Choosing Polypropylene Sandbags: What to Look For

When shopping for polypropylene sandbags, evaluate these factors:

### UV Treatment
Look for bags labeled "UV stabilized," "UV treated," or "UV resistant." Without UV treatment, polypropylene breaks down faster than burlap outdoors. UV treatment extends outdoor life from months to years.

### Weave Density
Higher weave density means fewer gaps in the fabric, which reduces fill material leaking through the bag walls. For sand fill, a tighter weave is preferable. For gravel, the weave density matters less.

### Seam Quality
Double-stitched or overlocked seams are significantly stronger than single-stitch. Check seam quality especially on budget bags — seam failure under fill pressure is one of the most common ways polypropylene bags fail.

### Bag Weight (Fabric GSM)
GSM (grams per square meter) indicates the fabric's weight and density. Higher GSM = heavier, stronger fabric. For flood protection sandbags, look for bags in the 75–120 GSM range for adequate strength.

### Color
Polypropylene sandbags come in white, natural, green, tan, and other colors. Color can indicate UV treatment (colored pigments sometimes include UV stabilizers) and can also serve practical purposes (e.g., visibility, camouflage). For most residential uses, color is a personal preference.

## Using Polypropylene Sandbags for Flood Protection

The filling and stacking process for polypropylene sandbags is identical to other types:

1. Fill bags 1/2 to 2/3 full with clean sand or sandy soil
2. Fold or tie the open end
3. Stack with the fold facing into the water source
4. Stagger joints between layers
5. Tamp each layer firmly
6. Maximum self-supporting height: 2–3 feet

## Storage and Disposal

**Storage**: Keep empty polypropylene sandbags in a cool, dark location away from direct sunlight. A storage bag, bin, or interior closet is ideal. Properly stored, UV-treated PP bags remain functional for a decade or more.

**Disposal**: Used polypropylene sandbags cannot be recycled through standard municipal recycling programs. Options include:
- Reusing bags if they remain structurally sound
- Landfill disposal (most areas)
- Some PP recycling programs (check with local waste management)
- Municipality debris collection following flood events

Do not leave polypropylene bags in place in the environment — they will not break down and can become debris hazards over time.

### What are polypropylene sandbags made of?

Polypropylene sandbags are made from woven strips of polypropylene plastic, a synthetic thermoplastic polymer. The woven fabric is cut and sewn into bags sized for sand or soil fill. Most flood-protection PP bags include UV stabilizers to extend outdoor lifespan.

### How long do polypropylene sandbags last?

UV-treated polypropylene sandbags can last 1–3 years outdoors and 10+ years when stored properly indoors. Non-UV-treated PP bags degrade faster in sunlight — often within 3–6 months of outdoor exposure.

### Are polypropylene sandbags waterproof?

Polypropylene bags are highly water-resistant — the fabric itself does not absorb water. However, woven PP bags are not fully watertight; water can seep slowly through the weave. They slow and redirect water flow rather than stopping it completely.

### Can polypropylene sandbags be reused?

Yes, if the bags remain structurally intact and were not contaminated with sewage or hazardous materials during use. Inspect each bag for tears, seam failures, and UV degradation before reusing. Contaminated bags should not be reused.

### What's the difference between woven and non-woven polypropylene sandbags?

Woven polypropylene is the standard for flood sandbags — it's made from interlocked plastic strips and is strong enough to hold sand under pressure. Non-woven polypropylene is made from bonded fibers and is more commonly used in filtration fabrics and geotextiles, not flood sandbag applications.

### Are polypropylene sandbags environmentally friendly?

Polypropylene is a plastic and is not biodegradable. It requires landfill or specialty recycling disposal. For environmentally sensitive applications, [burlap](/burlap-sandbags/), [jute](/jute-sandbags/), or other [biodegradable sandbag](/biodegradable-sandbags/) options are a better fit.
