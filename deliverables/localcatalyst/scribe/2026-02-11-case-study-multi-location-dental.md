## The Challenge

Desert Peak Dental Group operates four dental practices across the Phoenix metropolitan area, with locations in Scottsdale, Chandler, Tempe, and Gilbert. Founded by Dr. Priya Nair, the group had grown through clinical excellence and word-of-mouth referrals. But as patient acquisition costs rose and corporate dental chains like Aspen Dental and large DSO-backed practices poured money into digital marketing, Desert Peak found itself losing ground in local search.

When they contacted LocalCatalyst, the situation was concerning:

- **Inconsistent GBP profiles:** Each location had been set up at different times by different staff members. Categories, attributes, descriptions, photos, and service lists varied wildly. Two profiles hadn't been updated in over a year.
- **Weak review presence:** Only the Scottsdale location had a meaningful review count (47 reviews, 4.5 stars). The other three locations averaged just 18 reviews each, with ratings between 3.9 and 4.2.
- **Generic website:** Desert Peak had a single website with one set of service pages that made no mention of specific locations, neighborhoods, or geographic terms. There were no location-specific landing pages. A patient searching for "dentist in Chandler" would find nothing on the site that signaled relevance to Chandler.
- **No schema markup:** The site had zero structured data --- no LocalBusiness schema, no Dentist schema, no Service schema, no Review schema. Google had to guess at the entity relationships between the practice, its locations, and its services.
- **Dominant competitors:** The Phoenix metro dental market included well-funded DSO groups with dedicated marketing teams, established solo practices with 500+ reviews, and national franchises with aggressive local SEO playbooks.

## The Strategy

Multi-location local SEO requires a fundamentally different approach than single-location optimization. Each location must establish its own distinct local identity in Google's eyes while maintaining brand cohesion across the group. Our CATALYST methodology was applied to all four locations simultaneously, with location-specific customizations at every stage.

### Months 1-2: Foundation Building

**Individual GBP Optimization:**

We treated each location's Google Business Profile as a standalone project:

- Standardized primary and secondary categories across all 4 profiles (Dentist, Cosmetic Dentist, Pediatric Dentist, Emergency Dental Service)
- Wrote unique, keyword-rich business descriptions for each location incorporating neighborhood and city names
- Updated service lists to reflect the full range of offerings at each location (some locations offered services others didn't --- implants at Scottsdale, orthodontics at Chandler)
- Uploaded 30+ high-quality, geo-tagged photos per location: exterior signage, interior treatment rooms, team photos, before/after cases (with patient consent)
- Established location-specific GBP post calendars (2 posts per week per location) featuring seasonal dental tips, team spotlights, and service highlights
- Set up and optimized the Q&A section for each profile with pre-seeded questions covering insurance, hours, emergency availability, and new patient processes

**Citation Audit & Cleanup:**

A comprehensive citation audit across all four locations revealed significant inconsistencies:

- Location 1 (Scottsdale): 14 inconsistencies --- mostly correct, but old suite number on 9 directories
- Location 2 (Chandler): 23 inconsistencies --- previous phone number still listed on 15 platforms
- Location 3 (Tempe): 31 inconsistencies --- the most problematic, with a former address (the practice had moved 2 years prior) still appearing on 22 directories
- Location 4 (Gilbert): 19 inconsistencies --- opened most recently, many directories still missing listings entirely

We submitted corrections to all major data aggregators, manually updated high-authority directories, claimed unclaimed listings, merged duplicates, and created new listings on directories where locations were missing entirely.

**Technical SEO Overhaul:**

- Implemented LocalBusiness and Dentist schema markup for each location, with proper `@id` references and parent organization relationship
- Added Service schema for each service offered at each location
- Fixed crawl errors, broken internal links, and redirect chains identified in the technical audit
- Improved Core Web Vitals: LCP from 4.1s to 2.0s through image optimization, theme streamlining, and caching configuration
- Implemented proper hreflang-style internal linking between location pages (not hreflang literally, but clear navigational cross-linking)

### Months 3-4: Content Authority & Review Momentum

**Location-Specific Landing Pages:**

This was the highest-impact content initiative. We built dedicated landing pages for each location that served as local authority hubs:

- **4 location hub pages** (e.g., "/locations/scottsdale-dentist/") --- each 1,500+ words covering the specific location's team, services, neighborhood context, parking/transit information, insurance accepted, and patient testimonials specific to that office
- **Location-specific service pages** for high-value services --- rather than one generic "dental implants" page, we created "/scottsdale/dental-implants/", "/chandler/dental-implants/", etc., each with unique content addressing the specific competitive landscape and patient demographics of that area
- Total: 28 new pages across all four locations (4 hub pages + 6 service pages per location)

**Service Page Expansion:**

The existing generic service pages were rewritten as comprehensive pillar content (1,200-1,800 words each) and repositioned as the parent pages in a hub-and-spoke internal linking model. Location-specific service pages linked up to these pillar pages, and pillar pages linked down to each location variant.

**Review Generation Across All 4 Locations:**

We implemented a unified review generation system:

- Automated post-appointment SMS and email review requests customized per location
- Staff training on in-office review request timing and language
- Review response protocol: every review (positive and negative) received a personalized response within 24 hours
- Monthly review velocity targets set for each location based on competitive benchmarks

## Key Takeaways for Multi-Location Businesses

1. **Each location needs its own local SEO identity.** A single generic website cannot compete against location-specific content. Google rewards relevance, and relevance is local.

2. **Citation inconsistencies multiply across locations.** When you have four locations, citation problems don't add --- they compound. A systematic audit across all locations simultaneously is essential.

3. **Review generation must be systematized, not ad hoc.** Staff-driven, post-appointment review requests at scale produced consistent growth across all four locations. The practices that "try to remember to ask" never catch up.

4. **GBP is not set-and-forget.** Weekly posting, photo updates, Q&A management, and review responses signal to Google that each location is an active, engaged business. This matters for Map Pack ranking.

5. **Organic cost per lead demolishes paid in local dental.** At $12 per organic lead vs. $89 per PPC lead, the math is overwhelming. Organic SEO investment pays for itself many times over once rankings are established.
