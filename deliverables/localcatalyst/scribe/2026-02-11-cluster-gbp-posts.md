## Google Business Profile Posts: How to Use Posting to Boost Visibility and Engagement

Google Business Profile posts are one of the most underutilized features in local SEO. While most businesses focus on reviews and basic profile setup, posting represents a direct channel for communicating with potential customers within Google Search and Maps — and it sends active engagement signals that contribute to your local ranking authority. Our [Google Business Profile management services](/google-business-profile-management/) include strategic posting as a core component because the data consistently shows that active profiles outperform passive ones.

A weekly posting cadence is the minimum standard for competitive local SEO in 2026. Yet the majority of local businesses either never post or post sporadically without strategy. This represents an opportunity for businesses willing to commit to consistent, optimized posting.

## Types of GBP Posts

Google offers four post types, each serving a different purpose. An effective posting strategy rotates across all four to maintain variety and address different customer engagement objectives.

### What's New (Update Posts)

Update posts are the most versatile format. They support up to 1,500 characters of text, a photo or video, and an optional call-to-action button. Update posts remain visible on your profile for six months (previously they expired after seven days — Google extended this in 2024).

**Best uses for update posts:**
- Announce new services or service expansions
- Share company news, team additions, or certifications
- Highlight seasonal offerings or availability
- Promote blog content or resource guides
- Share industry tips or advice that demonstrates expertise

### Event Posts

Event posts include a title, start/end date, and optional time, description, photo, and CTA button. They remain visible until the event ends.

**Best uses for event posts:**
- Open houses, grand openings, or community events
- Webinars, workshops, or educational sessions
- Holiday-specific promotions with defined end dates
- Seasonal service windows (e.g., "Spring HVAC Tune-Up Special — March 1-31")

### Offer Posts

Offer posts include a title, start/end date, optional coupon code, link, terms, and photo. They display with a distinctive "View offer" tag that increases click-through rates.

**Best uses for offer posts:**
- Percentage discounts or dollar-off promotions
- First-time customer specials
- Referral incentives
- Bundle deals (e.g., "Free AC inspection with any plumbing service")
- Seasonal clearance or end-of-year promotions

### Product Posts

Product posts are part of the GBP Products feature (available for applicable business types). They include a product name, category, price, description, and photo. Products remain visible indefinitely and appear in a dedicated "Products" tab on your profile.

**Best uses for product posts:**
- Physical products with defined prices
- Service packages with fixed pricing
- Featured or seasonal product highlights

## Optimal Posting Frequency

The minimum competitive posting frequency is once per week. Our data across hundreds of local business profiles shows the following pattern:

- **No posting** — Profile signals stagnation. No engagement benefit. Baseline performance.
- **1 post per week** — Meaningful engagement signal. Demonstrates active business operation. Minimum recommended cadence.
- **2-3 posts per week** — Optimal range for most businesses. Provides consistent freshness signals without content fatigue.
- **Daily posting** — Marginal additional benefit. Risk of content quality declining if the volume exceeds your ability to produce genuinely useful content.

Consistency matters more than volume. A business that posts once per week, every week, for 12 months will outperform a business that posts daily for three weeks and then stops. Google's algorithm values sustained engagement patterns over bursts of activity.

## GBP Post Best Practices

### Image Optimization

Every post should include an image. Posts with images receive significantly higher engagement than text-only posts.

**Image specifications:**
- Minimum resolution: 720 x 540 pixels (4:3 aspect ratio recommended)
- Format: JPG or PNG
- File size: Under 5 MB (optimize for fast loading)
- Content: Original photos of your business, team, work, or products — never stock photography

Use bright, well-lit, high-contrast images. The post thumbnail in search results is small, so images need to be immediately recognizable and visually compelling even at reduced size. For more on visual content strategy, see our [GBP photos and videos guide](/google-business-profile-management/gbp-photos-videos/).

### Call-to-Action Buttons

Every post should include a CTA button. Available CTA options include:

- **Learn more** — Links to a relevant page on your website
- **Book** — Links to your booking/scheduling page
- **Order online** — Links to your online ordering system
- **Buy** — Links to a product purchase page
- **Sign up** — Links to a registration or email signup page
- **Call now** — Triggers a phone call (mobile users)

The CTA button transforms a passive post into an active conversion opportunity. Match the CTA to the post content — an offer post should use "Book" or "Order online," while an informational post should use "Learn more."

### Keyword Integration

GBP posts are indexed by Google and contribute to relevance signals. Including target keywords naturally in post content reinforces the topical associations between your profile and your target search queries.

**Effective keyword integration:**
- Include your primary service keyword in the first sentence when natural
- Mention your city or service area in posts about local topics
- Reference specific services using the same terminology as your [local keyword research](/local-seo-services/local-keyword-research/) targets
- Avoid keyword stuffing — posts should read naturally and provide genuine value

### Character Limits and Formatting

- Update, offer, and event posts support up to 1,500 characters
- Only the first 80-100 characters display before the "More" truncation on mobile
- Front-load the most important information and the primary keyword in the opening line
- Use line breaks to create scannable content
- Emojis are optional but can increase visual engagement in competitive feeds (use sparingly)

## Post Templates by Industry

Effective GBP posts follow proven structures. Here are templates adapted for common local business categories:

### Home Services (Plumbing, HVAC, Electrical)

**Seasonal tip post:** "Is your [system] ready for [season]? Here are 3 signs your [equipment] needs professional attention before [weather event] hits [city]. Our certified technicians are booking [service] appointments now — schedule yours before the rush."

**Completed project post:** "Just completed a [project type] for a homeowner in [neighborhood/city]. [Brief description of the work and outcome]. If you need [service type] in [city/area], our team is ready to help."

### Medical/Dental

**Service spotlight post:** "Did you know we offer [specific service]? [Brief description of the procedure and its benefits]. Dr. [Name] has performed over [number] [procedures] for patients throughout [city]. Schedule your consultation today."

**Patient education post:** "[Common question about health topic]? Our [city] team explains what you need to know about [topic] and when to seek professional care."

### Restaurants/Food Service

**Weekly feature post:** "This week's special at [Business Name]: [Description of featured dish/promotion]. Available [dates/times] for dine-in and takeout. [City] foodies — don't miss this one."

### Professional Services (Legal, Financial, Consulting)

**Authority post:** "[Recent industry development or regulation change] affects [city/state] [type of client]. Here's what our [city] [practice type] team recommends you do now to protect your [interests/investments/rights]."

## How Posts Impact Rankings and Engagement

GBP posts contribute to local rankings through several mechanisms:

**Direct ranking signals:**
- Post content is indexed and creates relevance associations between your profile and target keywords
- Posting frequency signals active business engagement
- Post interactions (clicks, CTA actions) generate behavioral signals

**Indirect ranking benefits:**
- Posts drive website traffic, which can improve your site's engagement metrics
- Posts can generate phone calls and direction requests, contributing to behavioral signals
- Consistent posting increases profile dwell time as users scroll through your post history

**Customer engagement impact:**
- Posts increase the surface area of your GBP, giving searchers more reasons to engage
- Offer posts drive direct conversions through promotional incentives
- Informational posts build trust and demonstrate expertise before the customer ever visits your website

## Measuring Post Performance

GBP provides limited native analytics for posts, but meaningful measurement is possible:

- **Post views** — Available in GBP Insights. Track trends over time rather than absolute numbers.
- **CTA clicks** — GBP reports clicks on your call-to-action buttons. Use UTM parameters on CTA links to track post-driven website traffic in Google Analytics.
- **Phone calls** — Calls initiated from "Call now" CTAs are tracked in GBP Insights.
- **Correlation with ranking changes** — Track geo-grid rankings alongside posting cadence to identify correlations between posting activity and ranking improvements.

We review post performance monthly and adjust content strategy based on which post types, topics, and formats generate the highest engagement in your specific market.

## Common GBP Posting Mistakes

Avoid these errors that reduce posting effectiveness:

1. **Inconsistent posting** — Starting strong and then abandoning the cadence. Set a sustainable frequency and maintain it.
2. **Text-only posts** — Posts without images receive dramatically less engagement. Always include a visual.
3. **No CTA** — Every post should give the viewer a next step. A post without a CTA is a missed conversion opportunity.
4. **Generic content** — "Happy Monday from our team!" posts provide no value, no relevance signals, and no engagement motivation. Every post should inform, offer, or demonstrate expertise.
5. **Ignoring post types** — Using only update posts when offers, events, and products would be more appropriate for the content.
6. **Not tracking results** — Without measurement, you cannot optimize. Track post performance and iterate on what works.
7. **Over-promotional content** — Every post being a hard sell creates fatigue. Follow the 80/20 rule: 80% value-driven content, 20% promotional.

## Automation and Efficiency

For businesses that struggle to maintain a consistent posting schedule, several approaches can help:

- **Content batching** — Write 4-8 posts in a single session, then schedule them for publication over the coming weeks.
- **Scheduling tools** — Third-party tools (Publer, Sendible, SocialPilot) allow you to schedule GBP posts in advance. Google's native scheduling is limited, but these tools integrate through the GBP API.
- **Template rotation** — Build a library of post templates that can be refreshed with new details each cycle. The templates above provide starting points.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long do GBP posts stay visible?

Update posts remain visible on your profile for six months. Event and offer posts remain visible until their specified end date. Product posts remain visible indefinitely. After expiration, posts are not deleted — they move to a "Past posts" section accessible from your profile. The ranking signals from expired posts diminish over time but do not disappear immediately, which is another reason consistent new posting matters.

### Do GBP posts help with Google Maps rankings?

Yes. GBP posts contribute to the engagement and relevance signals that influence Google Maps rankings. The keyword content in posts creates relevance associations, and the act of consistent posting signals an active, legitimate business. While posts alone will not propel a poorly optimized profile into the [Map Pack](/local-seo-services/google-maps-ranking/), they are a meaningful contributing factor within a comprehensive optimization strategy.

### Can I include links in my GBP posts?

You can include a link through the CTA button, which supports URLs to any page on your website. In-text URLs within the post body are supported but are not clickable — they display as plain text. For tracking purposes, always use the CTA button for your link and add UTM parameters to measure traffic from GBP posts in Google Analytics.

### What is the best time to publish GBP posts?

There is no universal best time. Unlike social media, GBP posts are not displayed in a chronological feed — they appear on your profile whenever a user views it in search results. What matters is consistency of cadence, not time of day. That said, posting earlier in the week ensures your profile has fresh content for weekend searchers, when many local searches peak.

### Should I use the same content on GBP posts and social media?

You can repurpose themes and topics, but we recommend customizing content for each platform. GBP posts should be keyword-optimized and focused on services and local relevance — they serve a search visibility function, not a social engagement function. Social media posts can be more casual, personality-driven, and community-focused. The audience mindset is different: GBP viewers are actively searching for a service, while social media followers are passively consuming content.
