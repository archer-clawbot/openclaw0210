## Meet the Agents

### Silas — Strategy and Auditing

Silas runs the [CATALYST methodology](/platform/methodology/). Every audit, every strategic recommendation, and every prioritization decision runs through Silas. When you order an SEO audit, Silas coordinates the data collection, analyzes every ranking factor against your local competitors, identifies gaps, and builds the prioritized roadmap that tells you exactly what to fix and in what order.

### Scout — Research and Reconnaissance

Scout handles the intelligence work: keyword research, competitor analysis, site crawling, and data collection. Before any deliverable is built, Scout has already mapped the competitive landscape, identified the highest-opportunity keywords, and cataloged every technical issue on your site. Scout feeds structured data to every other agent in the system.

### Scribe — Content Writing

Scribe produces the actual content: service pages, blog posts, landing pages, location pages, and supporting content. Every piece is written against keyword targets, structured for on-page SEO, and formatted for immediate implementation. Scribe does not write generic content and hope it ranks. Scribe writes to a specific keyword strategy informed by Scout's research and Silas's prioritization.

### Razor — Copywriting and Persuasion

Razor handles the words that convert: ad copy, calls to action, meta descriptions, conversion-focused landing page copy, and headline optimization. Where Scribe writes for search engines and readers, Razor writes for the moment of decision. The two work in sequence — Scribe builds the page, Razor sharpens the conversion points.

### Wrench — Technical SEO

Wrench handles everything under the hood: schema markup, redirect chains, site speed optimization, Core Web Vitals, crawlability, indexation issues, XML sitemaps, robots.txt configuration, and every other technical factor that affects how search engines process your site. When your audit identifies technical issues, Wrench builds the implementation plan.

### Herald — Google Business Profile

Herald manages your GBP presence: profile optimization, post creation, attribute management, category strategy, photo optimization, Q&A monitoring, and ongoing maintenance. GBP is the single highest-visibility asset for most local businesses, and Herald treats it with the dedicated focus it demands.

### Ghost — Off-Site SEO and Link Building

Ghost works the off-site signals: backlink acquisition, digital PR, local link building, resource page placements, and citation strategy. Ghost identifies opportunities, evaluates link quality, and builds the off-site authority that search engines use to determine which businesses deserve the top positions.

### Citadel — Security and Compliance

Citadel monitors for security vulnerabilities, ensures HTTPS implementation, checks for malware and spam signals, and handles the compliance factors that can tank rankings overnight if neglected. Most businesses never think about security until something breaks. Citadel makes sure nothing breaks.

### Lookout — Competitor Intelligence

Lookout watches your competitors continuously: tracking their ranking changes, new content, backlink acquisitions, GBP updates, and strategic shifts. When a competitor makes a move, Lookout flags it so the system can respond. When an opportunity opens up because a competitor dropped the ball, Lookout identifies it before anyone else does.

### Mozi — Business Strategy

Mozi handles the strategic layer above SEO: market positioning, service area expansion planning, revenue opportunity analysis, and resource allocation recommendations. Mozi ensures that the SEO work is aligned with actual business objectives, not just vanity metrics.

### Builder — Web Development

Builder constructs [SEO-first WordPress sites](/services/website-build/) from the ground up: optimized theme selection, site architecture, page speed configuration, mobile responsiveness, schema implementation, and every technical element that determines whether a site is built to rank or built to look pretty on a designer's portfolio.

### And More

The full system includes 18 agents across every SEO discipline. Some handle specialized functions like review response drafting, local content localization, and algorithm update analysis. The exact roster evolves as we identify new specializations that improve deliverable quality.

## How the Agents Work Together

No agent works in isolation. Every deliverable is the product of multiple agents collaborating through the [CATALYST methodology](/platform/methodology/).

Take an SEO audit as an example:

1. **Scout** crawls your site, collects technical data, and maps the competitive landscape.
2. **Silas** analyzes the data, scores every ranking factor, and identifies the gaps between you and the top competitors.
3. **Wrench** evaluates the technical findings and generates specific implementation recommendations.
4. **Herald** audits your GBP profile against best practices and competitor profiles.
5. **Ghost** analyzes your backlink profile and identifies off-site weaknesses.
6. **Silas** synthesizes everything into a prioritized action plan ranked by impact and effort.

The result is a comprehensive audit that covers more ground in hours than a human team would cover in weeks. Not because the AI cuts corners — because 18 specialists working in parallel are simply faster than one person working sequentially.

## Why AI Agents, Not AI Tools

There is an important distinction between an AI tool and an AI agent.

An AI tool generates output when you push a button. You provide the prompt, you evaluate the result, you decide what to do with it. The quality depends entirely on your expertise.

An AI agent operates within a defined methodology, uses structured data, follows quality standards, and coordinates with other agents to produce a finished deliverable. You do not need to know how to write a prompt. You do not need to evaluate raw output. The system handles that.

LocalCatalyst agents are not ChatGPT with an SEO prompt. They are purpose-built systems running a tested methodology on structured data.

That distinction is why the deliverables are good enough to stake our business on.
