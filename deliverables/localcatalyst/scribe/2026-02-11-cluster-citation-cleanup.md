## Why Citation Cleanup Matters

NAP inconsistency — your Name, Address, and Phone number appearing differently across directories — is one of the most common and most correctable ranking suppressors in local SEO.

Google's algorithms cross-reference your business data across every source they can find. When that data conflicts, Google's confidence in your business information decreases. Lower confidence means lower rankings. It's that simple.

### The Scale of the Problem

The average local business we audit has inconsistent NAP data across **40% or more** of its existing citations. That's not a minor issue — it's a systematic trust problem that affects every local query your business should appear for.

### How Inconsistencies Accumulate

Most businesses don't create citation inconsistencies intentionally. They accumulate over time through entirely normal business events:

- **Address changes** — You moved offices, suites, or expanded to a new location. Google and Yelp got updated. The other 30-50 directories didn't.
- **Phone number changes** — New phone system, different tracking numbers for marketing campaigns, a previous agency that set up call tracking and never cleaned it up.
- **Business name evolution** — "Smith Electric" became "Smith Electrical Services" on some listings but not others. Or the legal name appears on some platforms while the DBA appears on others.
- **Data aggregator contamination** — A data aggregator (Data Axle, Foursquare, Localeze, Factual) has an old record. That old record continuously feeds incorrect information to dozens of downstream directories. You can correct the downstream listings, but they get overwritten when the aggregator pushes another data update.
- **Unauthorized listings** — Directories auto-generate profiles from aggregator data. A previous marketing agency created listings and never transferred access. An employee set up listings that nobody tracks.
- **Format inconsistencies** — "Suite 200" vs. "Ste 200" vs. "#200" vs. "Unit 200." Google's algorithms may or may not reconcile these, and the uncertainty itself is a trust drag.

### The Compounding Effect

Citation inconsistency isn't a static problem — it compounds. One incorrect record in a major data aggregator can propagate to 50+ downstream directories over the course of months. A single source of bad upstream data creates an expanding web of inconsistency that gets harder to fix the longer it persists.

This is why cleanup before building is essential. It's also why citation management is ongoing — we cover this in depth on our [citation building hub page](/citation-building-services/).

## Common Citation Problems We Fix

### Wrong or Outdated Addresses

The most common and most damaging inconsistency. An old address on a high-authority directory tells Google your business might not actually be where you say it is. We've seen businesses ranking poorly despite excellent on-page SEO and [GBP optimization](/google-business-profile-management/) — because 15 directories still listed their previous address from two years ago.

### Incorrect Phone Numbers

Old phone numbers, disconnected lines, and call tracking numbers that were never cleaned up. Phone number inconsistencies are especially problematic because Google uses phone number matching as a primary signal for connecting citations to your Google Business Profile.

### Business Name Variations

"Bob's HVAC" on Google, "Bob's Heating and Air Conditioning" on Yelp, "Robert's HVAC LLC" on the BBB. Each variation fragments your citation profile and dilutes the NAP consistency signal.

### Duplicate Listings

Two or more listings for the same business on the same platform. Duplicates cause confusion for both Google's algorithms and potential customers. Google may struggle to identify which listing is authoritative, potentially suppressing both.

### Closed Location Listings Still Live

If your business previously operated at a different location, those old listings may still be live and active — showing an address you no longer occupy, a phone number you no longer answer, or hours you no longer keep. These ghost listings directly compete with your current, correct listings for Google's trust.

### Category Mismatches

Wrong or overly broad business categories on directory listings. A dental practice listed as "Healthcare" instead of "Dentist" on 20 directories misses category relevance signals that support rankings.

## Our Cleanup Process

### Phase 1: Comprehensive Citation Audit

We scan every major data source to build a complete picture of your citation profile:

- **Major directories** — Google, Yelp, Bing, Apple Maps, Facebook, BBB, and 50+ others
- **Data aggregators** — Data Axle, Foursquare, Localeze/Neustar, Factual
- **Industry-specific directories** — Platforms relevant to your vertical (Healthgrades, Avvo, HomeAdvisor, etc.)
- **Local directories** — Regional and hyper-local platforms in your service area
- **Social platforms** — Facebook, LinkedIn, Instagram (business profiles carry NAP data)

For every listing we find, we record: the platform, your listed NAP data, category, URL, claimed/unclaimed status, and any additional information that deviates from your canonical business data.

**Deliverable:** A complete citation audit report showing every listing, every inconsistency, and a prioritized correction plan.

### Phase 2: Prioritized Corrections

Not all corrections carry equal weight. We prioritize by impact:

**Priority 1: Data aggregators.** Correcting the four major aggregators stops bad data from propagating downstream. This is the single highest-leverage action in citation cleanup.

**Priority 2: Tier 1 platforms.** Google Business Profile, Yelp, Bing Places, Apple Maps, Facebook, BBB. These carry the most individual authority and visibility.

**Priority 3: Industry-specific directories.** Platforms relevant to your vertical, prioritized by domain authority and traffic.

**Priority 4: General and local directories.** Remaining inconsistent listings, corrected in order of authority.

### Phase 3: Correction Submissions

Every directory has a different process for corrections:

- **Claimed listings:** We log into the platform and update information directly
- **Unclaimed listings:** We claim the listing first (verification methods vary — phone, email, postcard, or video verification), then update
- **Aggregator submissions:** We submit corrections through each aggregator's data correction process and follow up until the update propagates
- **Duplicate removal requests:** We submit removal/merge requests through each platform's duplicate resolution process

Some corrections take effect within 48 hours. Others take 4-8 weeks. We track every submission and follow up on any that haven't resolved within expected timeframes.

### Phase 4: Verification and Monitoring

Submitting corrections isn't enough — we verify they take effect:

- **Re-scan every corrected listing** within 2-4 weeks of submission
- **Flag corrections that didn't take** and resubmit with escalated requests
- **Monitor aggregator data feeds** for regression (aggregators occasionally revert to old data)
- **Track NAP consistency score** over time — from initial audit baseline to 95%+ target

## When to Do Cleanup vs. Fresh Build

If your business has fewer than 20 existing citations and minimal inconsistency, a fresh citation build from scratch may be more efficient than a cleanup process. We'll make this determination during the audit.

**Cleanup is recommended when:**
- You have 30+ existing citations with 20%+ inconsistency rate
- You've changed addresses, phone numbers, or business name in the past 3 years
- Your rankings have declined without an obvious cause
- A previous agency managed your citations and you don't have access or records

**Fresh build is recommended when:**
- You're a new business with minimal existing listings
- You have fewer than 20 total citations
- Your existing citations are mostly accurate

For most established local businesses, cleanup + building is the right approach — clean the foundation first, then build strategically on top. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I know if I need citation cleanup?

If you've been in business for more than 2 years, changed locations or phone numbers, or had a previous marketing agency manage your listings, you almost certainly need cleanup. The only way to know for sure is a citation audit. Our [free SEO audit](/free-seo-audit/) includes a citation health snapshot that reveals your current consistency score.

### Can I do citation cleanup myself?

Technically yes, but it's extremely time-consuming. Manually finding, claiming, and correcting 50-100+ listings across platforms with different processes, verification methods, and update timelines requires 40-60+ hours of work. Most business owners find the time investment isn't worth it when the cost of professional cleanup delivers results faster and more thoroughly.

### What happens if I don't fix my citations?

Inconsistencies continue suppressing your rankings. Worse, they compound — aggregator data continues propagating incorrect information to new directories, and your inconsistency rate grows over time. We've seen businesses lose Map Pack visibility entirely due to severe citation inconsistency that accumulated over 2-3 years without intervention.

### Will citation cleanup help if my competitors have more citations?

Yes, because cleanup and building are sequential. First, we fix your existing citations to eliminate the suppression effect. Then, we build new citations to close the quantity and source gap with competitors. Cleanup is the foundation — building is the growth strategy. Both are covered in our [citation building services](/citation-building-services/).

### How often should citations be audited?

We recommend a comprehensive audit every 6 months, with monthly monitoring scans between audits. Business changes (new locations, phone numbers, hours, services) should trigger immediate targeted updates across your entire citation profile.
