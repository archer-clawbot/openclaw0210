## Content Refresh and Pruning Strategy

Publishing content is only half the job. Maintaining it is the other half.

### Content Refresh

Search results change constantly. A page that ranked #2 six months ago might be #8 today because competitors published better content, Google updated its algorithm, or the information became outdated.

We monitor every piece of content we create and proactively refresh pages when:

- Rankings drop below target positions
- Traffic declines for more than 2 consecutive months
- New competitors enter the top 5 for target keywords
- Information becomes outdated (statistics, best practices, regulations)
- Google's SERP features change for the target query

**Our content refresh process:**
1. Re-analyze the current SERP to understand what's changed
2. Update outdated information and statistics
3. Expand coverage of subtopics that new top-ranking pages include
4. Add new internal links to recently created content
5. Optimize for any new SERP features (AI Overviews, featured snippets)
6. Republish with updated date and promote

### Content Pruning

Not all content deserves to stay. Pages that generate no traffic, rank for no keywords, and have no backlinks actively hurt your site by diluting crawl budget and quality signals.

We audit your full content portfolio quarterly and recommend pruning (removing, consolidating, or redirecting) content that:

- Has generated zero organic sessions in 6+ months
- Targets keywords that overlap with higher-performing pages
- Falls below minimum quality standards
- No longer aligns with your business offerings

Learn more about this process in our guide: [Content Pruning for SEO](/learn/content-pruning-seo/).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does it take for SEO content to start ranking?

It depends on your site's existing authority, the competitiveness of your market, and the keywords you're targeting. For long-tail keywords with moderate competition, we typically see initial rankings within 4-8 weeks of publication. For more competitive head terms, expect 3-6 months as your topical authority builds. Our pillar-cluster approach accelerates this by building authority systematically rather than hoping individual pages rank in isolation.

### How much content do I need per month?

There's no universal answer — it depends on your content gaps, competitive landscape, and budget. Most of our local business clients publish 4-8 pieces per month during the first 6 months (the foundation-building phase), then scale to 2-4 pieces per month for ongoing growth and maintenance. Quality always beats quantity. Four strategically planned, well-executed pieces will outperform 20 generic blog posts every time.

### Do you write the content or do we need to provide it?

We handle everything. Our team writes all content based on the strategy we develop together. We'll interview you or your team for subject matter expertise and E-E-A-T signals — your real-world experience is what makes the content authoritative. But you don't need to write a word unless you want to.

### How is this different from hiring a freelance writer?

A freelance writer produces content. We build content systems. The difference is strategy, architecture, and integration. A freelance writer doesn't conduct keyword research, build pillar-cluster architectures, implement schema markup, optimize for AI Overviews, track rankings, refresh declining content, or integrate content with your broader [SEO strategy](/services/). We do all of that. The content itself is just one component of the larger system.

### Can you work with content we've already published?

Absolutely. Our content audit evaluates every existing page and identifies what to keep, what to optimize, what to consolidate, and what to remove. Often, existing content just needs strategic optimization — better keyword targeting, improved internal linking, expanded coverage, and proper schema markup — to start performing. We never throw away good content unnecessarily.

### How do you handle E-E-A-T for local businesses?

E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) is baked into every piece of content. For local businesses, this means: incorporating real project examples and case studies (Experience), leveraging your team's credentials and certifications (Expertise), building topical authority through comprehensive coverage (Authoritativeness), and including transparent pricing, real reviews, and accurate information (Trustworthiness). We also ensure proper author attribution and business entity signals across your content.

### What if I'm in a "boring" industry? Does content still work?

There's no such thing as a boring industry in SEO — only boring content. Your customers have questions, concerns, and problems they're searching Google to solve. A pest control company might think their industry is boring, but "how to get rid of carpenter ants in kitchen" gets 2,400 searches per month. An HVAC company might not think people want to read about air conditioning, but "AC not blowing cold air" gets 12,000 monthly searches. We find the questions your customers actually ask and create content that answers them better than anyone else.

### How do you measure ROI on content?

We track organic traffic, keyword rankings, and — most importantly — conversions and revenue attribution. Every piece of content has a purpose, and we measure whether it's fulfilling that purpose. For commercial content (service pages, location pages), we track calls, form submissions, and appointments directly. For informational content (blog posts, guides), we track assisted conversions — how often that content was part of a customer's journey before they converted. You'll see all of this in your monthly report.
