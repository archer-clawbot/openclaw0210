## Our Review Generation System

We build and deploy multi-channel review generation systems tailored to your business type, customer journey, and communication preferences. Every element is designed to minimize friction for your customer while maximizing conversion to published reviews.

### Post-Service Email and SMS Sequences

Timing is the single most important variable in review generation. Our data shows review likelihood drops 40% after 24 hours and 70% after 72 hours. The experience is freshest — and the customer's willingness highest — within the first 1-2 hours after service completion.

We deploy automated sequences triggered by service completion in your CRM, scheduling software, or POS system:

**Message 1 (1-2 hours post-service):** Personalized thank-you message referencing the specific service performed, with a direct link to your Google review page. SMS outperforms email by 2-3x for review generation — open rates of 95%+ versus 25-35% for email.

**Message 2 (48 hours later, if no review posted):** A brief follow-up for customers who opened but did not act on the first message. This single follow-up captures an additional 5-8% of customers. We never send more than two messages — persistence beyond this point creates negative brand associations.

We A/B test message copy, timing windows, sender names, and delivery channels continuously. For a veterinary clinic in Austin, switching from a 24-hour email delay to a 2-hour SMS with the veterinarian's name included increased their review generation rate from 4% to 19% of appointments.

### Review Funnels With Intelligent Routing

A review funnel is a simple, mobile-optimized landing page where customers rate their experience before being directed to a review platform.

Here is how it works:

1. Customer taps the link in your SMS or email
2. They land on a branded page asking: "How was your experience?"
3. Customers who indicate a positive experience (4-5 stars) are directed to your Google review page with a pre-formatted link that opens directly to the review input
4. Customers who indicate a negative experience (1-3 stars) are directed to a private feedback form that comes directly to your team

This is not review gating — a practice that violates Google's policies. Every customer can leave a public review at any point. The funnel simply creates a natural pathway that routes negative experiences to private resolution first. This gives your team the opportunity to address the issue before it becomes a public complaint — and in many cases, the resolved issue becomes a positive review instead.

### QR Codes and In-Store Strategies

For businesses with physical locations — dental offices, restaurants, salons, auto repair shops, gyms, retail stores — we design and produce review signage with QR codes linked directly to your Google review page.

**Strategic placement matters:**

- **Checkout counters:** Capture customers at the moment of satisfaction (they just received their service)
- **Waiting rooms:** Give patients or clients something constructive to do while waiting
- **Service bays:** Auto repair and salon chairs where customers have downtime
- **Table tents:** Restaurant tables during the post-meal period
- **Technician leave-behinds:** Cards that home service technicians hand to the customer at job completion

QR code reviews tend to be shorter but still count toward your review volume and velocity metrics. We track QR-specific conversion rates and optimize placement based on performance data.

### Staff Training for Natural Review Requests

Your front-line employees are your most effective review generators. A genuine, in-person ask from the person who delivered the service converts at 2-3x the rate of any automated system.

But most employees feel awkward asking for reviews. They do not know when to ask, how to phrase it, or what to do if the customer hesitates.

We provide:

- **Scripting that sounds natural:** Not "Please leave us a 5-star review" but "It would really help our small business if you could share your experience on Google — we can text you the link right now."
- **Timing training:** Ask after a positive interaction signal (the customer thanks you, expresses satisfaction, compliments the work) — never during service or before the outcome is clear
- **Handling hesitation:** If a customer declines, the trained response is "No problem at all — we appreciate your business." Zero pressure.
- **Role-specific scripts:** A dental hygienist's ask is different from an HVAC technician's ask is different from a restaurant server's ask. We customize for the interaction context.

For home service businesses, we provide technician-specific leave-behind cards with QR codes and short URLs that make the follow-through effortless.

## Ethical Guidelines: What We Never Do

Our review generation practices are fully compliant with Google's review policies and FTC guidelines. This is non-negotiable.

**We never:**

- Offer incentives, discounts, or rewards in exchange for reviews
- Gate reviews (preventing negative reviewers from posting publicly)
- Write or draft reviews on behalf of customers
- Ask customers to mention specific keywords in their reviews
- Solicit reviews from people who were not actual customers
- Purchase reviews from any source

**We always:**

- Request reviews from genuine customers who received your service
- Direct customers to the platform of their choice (with Google as the primary recommendation)
- Allow every customer to post public reviews regardless of their sentiment
- Provide private feedback channels as an alternative, not a barrier
- Maintain complete transparency with business owners about our process

Ethical review generation is not just the right thing to do — it is the only sustainable approach. Google's algorithms are increasingly sophisticated at detecting fake and incentivized reviews. Businesses caught violating review policies face penalties ranging from review removal to complete listing suspension. We build review profiles that withstand scrutiny because they are entirely genuine.

## Review Velocity and Its Impact on Rankings

Google does not just count total reviews — it tracks how frequently you receive new ones. Review velocity is the rate at which new reviews are posted to your profile.

A business with 200 reviews that received its last one three months ago sends a very different signal than a business with 200 reviews that received 15 in the past month. Google interprets consistent review velocity as an indicator that the business is active, relevant, and consistently serving customers.

Our review generation systems are designed to produce **steady, sustainable velocity** — not spikes. A sudden surge of 50 reviews in one week looks suspicious to both Google and consumers. Our target is a consistent cadence: 8-20 new reviews per month for a typical local business, depending on customer volume.

This steady velocity also keeps your review profile fresh. Consumers pay more attention to recent reviews than historical ones. A 4.8-star average with the most recent review from six months ago raises doubts. A 4.7-star average with 12 reviews in the last 30 days signals a thriving, active business.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does it take to set up a review generation system?

We typically have your complete review generation system live within 10-14 business days. This includes CRM/scheduling software integration, SMS and email sequence creation, review funnel design and deployment, QR code signage design, and staff training. The first reviews from the automated system usually appear within the first week of activation.

### Is it legal to ask customers for reviews?

Yes. Asking customers to share their honest experience is perfectly legal and compliant with both Google's policies and FTC guidelines. What is not legal is incentivizing reviews (offering discounts or rewards), writing reviews on behalf of customers, or selectively preventing negative reviews from being posted. Our process is fully compliant with all platform policies and federal guidelines.

### What if we get negative reviews through the system?

Negative reviews are a natural part of any review profile — and they actually increase the credibility of your positive reviews. Our review funnel provides a private feedback pathway for dissatisfied customers, giving your team the chance to resolve issues before they become public. But we never prevent negative reviews from being posted. When they do appear, our negative review management process ensures a rapid, professional response that demonstrates accountability.

### Can you set up review generation for multiple locations?

Absolutely. Multi-location review generation is one of our specialties. Each location gets its own customized sequences, review funnels, and QR signage pointing to the correct Google Business Profile. We track performance by location and optimize independently, because what works for your downtown office may not work for your suburban location.

### What if my current rating is very low — will generating more reviews help?

Yes, but the timeline depends on your starting point. If your current issues are operational (consistently poor service leading to legitimate complaints), you need to fix the operational issues first — no review generation system can mask genuine service problems. If your low rating reflects the natural negative skew of organic reviews rather than actual service quality, generating reviews from your satisfied majority will steadily improve your rating. We model the exact math: how many reviews at what average rating are needed to reach your target, and how long that will take given realistic generation rates.
