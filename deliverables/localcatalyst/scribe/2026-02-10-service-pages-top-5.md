## What Are Local Citations?

### H2
`What Are Local Citations?`

A local citation is any online mention of your business name, address, and phone number (NAP). Citations can be structured or unstructured:

**Structured Citations**  
Listings on business directories like Google Business Profile, Yelp, Yellow Pages, Bing Places, and industry-specific directories.

**Unstructured Citations**  
Mentions of your business in blog posts, news articles, local event pages, or sponsorship listings.

**Why Citations Matter for Local SEO:**

1. **Google uses citations to verify your location.** If your NAP is consistent across 50+ directories, Google trusts that you're a real, legitimate business at that address.

2. **Citations are a ranking factor for the Map Pack.** Businesses with more accurate, consistent citations rank higher in local search results.

3. **Citations drive direct traffic.** People find your business on Yelp, Yellow Pages, and other directories — not just Google.

4. **Inconsistent citations hurt your rankings.** If Google finds 10 different phone numbers for your business across directories, it doesn't know which one is correct — so it ranks you lower.

---

## Why Local Citations Matter

### H2
`Why Your Business Needs Accurate Citations`

**1. NAP Consistency Is a Top Local Ranking Factor**

Google's local search algorithm heavily weights citation consistency. Businesses with accurate, consistent NAP data across 50+ directories rank higher than businesses with inconsistent or missing data.

**2. Duplicate Listings Confuse Google**

If you have three different Google Business Profiles (maybe from an old location, a misspelled name, or a previous owner), Google doesn't know which one is real. Duplicate listings dilute your rankings.

**3. Customers Call the Wrong Number**
