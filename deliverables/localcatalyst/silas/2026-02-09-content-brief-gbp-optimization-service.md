### Section 1: What Is GBP Optimization? (Educational)
**H2:** What Is Google Business Profile Optimization?

**Content:**

Your **Google Business Profile** (formerly Google My Business or GMB) is the profile that appears when someone searches for your business or services on Google Maps or Google Search.

**It includes:**
- Business name, address, phone (NAP)
- Business category and attributes
- Business description (750 characters)
- Services list
- Products tab
- Photos and videos
- Reviews
- Q&A section
- Posts (updates, offers, events)
- Website link
- Booking/messaging options

**Why It Matters:**  
Your GBP is the **#1 ranking factor** for appearing in the Map Pack (the 3-business showcase that appears for "near me" searches). If you're not optimizing every element, you're losing to competitors who are.

### Section 7: FAQ
**H2:** Google Business Profile Optimization FAQs

**Q: What's the difference between GMB, GBP, and Google My Business?**  
A: They're the same thing. Google rebranded "Google My Business" (GMB) to "Google Business Profile" (GBP) in 2021. We use GBP, but many still call it GMB.

**Q: How long does GBP optimization take to see results?**  
A: Quick wins (description, services, Q&A) can show ranking movement within 2-4 weeks. Sustained Map Pack domination (with reviews and posting) typically takes 8-12 weeks.

**Q: Can I optimize my GBP myself?**  
A: You can handle basics (claim, add photos, respond to reviews), but advanced tactics (entity co-citation engineering, Q&A seeding, AI Overview optimization) require expertise and time.

**Q: Do I need a physical location to have a GBP?**  
A: No. Service-area businesses (SABs) can have a GBP without displaying an address. You just hide your address and show your service area.

**Q: What if I have multiple locations?**  
A: Each location gets its own GBP. We optimize each individually with location-specific content while maintaining brand consistency.

**Q: How many posts should I publish per week?**  
A: Minimum 3x/week to signal an active profile. We recommend 3-5x/week for maximum impact.

**Q: Can negative reviews hurt my rankings?**  
A: Yes, but overall rating and review volume matter more than a few negative reviews. We have a negative review mitigation strategy to minimize damage.
